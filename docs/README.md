# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme project management documentation set. This README is the central entry point for team members and contributors who need a quick understanding of how work is initiated, planned, delivered, released, and continuously improved across projects.

OctoAcme follows a lightweight but structured lifecycle: initiation clarifies the business need, stakeholders, goals, and success metrics; planning breaks approved work into prioritized, estimable increments with acceptance criteria, dependencies, risks, and release milestones. This helps teams align early and move into delivery with clear ownership and expected outcomes.

Execution emphasizes transparent workflows and predictable communication. Teams track work through board stages such as Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, and Done, and use recurring standups, weekly syncs, demos, and stakeholder updates to surface progress, blockers, and decisions. Risks are managed through a maintained risk register and clear escalation paths from team-level triage to PM, Product Lead, and sponsor support when needed.

Quality is built into daily delivery and release practices. OctoAcme teams use unit and integration testing, release smoke tests, CI checks (including linting and security scanning), and manual QA where appropriate for feature acceptance. Releases require readiness checks, rollback planning, and post-deploy verification, while retrospectives turn lessons learned into owned action items that drive continuous improvement.
